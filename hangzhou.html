Discover Hangzhou

Journey through the poetic landscapes of Jiangnan

🌊 West Lake

West Lake is the heart of Hangzhou, celebrated as “Paradise on Earth.” Its scenic beauty blends with rich cultural heritage. Famous spots like Su Causeway, Three Pools Mirroring the Moon, and Leifeng Pagoda offer unique charm in every season. Walking, cycling, or boating here reveals the poetic essence of Jiangnan.

🏞 Qiandao Lake

Qiandao Lake, or “Thousand Island Lake,” is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and over 1,000 emerald islands. Known as “the world’s most beautiful waters,” it’s ideal for boat cruises and enjoying local organic fish cuisine. The lake’s vast expanse resembles a natural masterpiece.

🌿 Xixi Wetland

Xixi National Wetland Park is China’s first national wetland reserve, combining urban wetlands, farming traditions, and natural ecology. With winding waterways and abundant wildlife, it’s perfect for eco-tours, photography, and leisure walks. A tranquil escape within the city.

🕍 Lingyin Temple

Founded in the Eastern Jin Dynasty, Lingyin Temple is one of China’s most famous Buddhist temples. Its halls are filled with incense, while the nearby Feilai Peak features exquisite stone carvings. Visitors come to seek blessings and experience centuries of spiritual culture.

🚤 Wuzhen

Wuzhen is a classic Jiangnan water town, where ancient bridges, flowing canals, and Ming–Qing architecture create timeless charm. By day, boat rides reveal historic alleys; by night, lanterns reflect on the water, offering a magical atmosphere. A living museum of traditional life.

🏛 Liangzhu

The Liangzhu site showcases a 5,000-year-old civilization and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Ancient city ruins, jade artifacts, and water systems highlight the brilliance of Neolithic China. Walking through the park feels like traveling back to the dawn of Chinese civilization.
